Impacts of Human Trafficking

Human trafficking occurs everywhere, but people are mainly trafficked from lower-income to higher-income countries. Most victims of cross-border trafficking come from Africa, especially sub-Saharan Africa, and from South and East Asia.

Human trafficking is a widespread crime and a lucrative business

Human trafficking is one of the fastest-growing crimes, along with drugs and arms trafficking, and a highly profitable business, generating an estimated $150 billion in profits each year.

Human trafficking thrives on poverty, conflict, and climate change

Traffickers capitalize on desperation, disparity, and deficiency, targeting people who are vulnerable, marginalized, or in difficult situations, including irregular or smuggled migrants and those in urgent need of work.

Traffickers use deception and violence to exploit victims

Taking advantage of the high demand for cheap labor, commercial sex, or other services, criminals exploit weak governance and corrupt systems to carry out illegal activities. They may fraudulently promise a better life, offer exciting jobs, or use outright violence to coerce vulnerable people into exploitation.
